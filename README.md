# The Marblehead Independent's 2026 Town Meeting Guide

This repository contains the GitHub Pages site for **The Marblehead Independent's 2026 Town Meeting Guide**, a public-facing civic guide built to help Marblehead residents understand the issues, decisions and votes in front of them during the 2026 Annual Town Meeting cycle.

The project is designed as a newsroom-ready reference that turns complicated town documents, financial questions and warrant language into a more readable web experience. Instead of asking readers to piece together information from agendas, PDFs, committee discussions and public debate on their own, this guide brings the core context into one place.

At its heart, the site is a public-service journalism project. It aims to make Town Meeting easier to follow, easier to navigate and easier to understand for residents who want a quick overview as well as for readers who want to dig deeper into individual articles and major policy questions.

## What This Repository Is

This is a lightweight static website intended for publication through GitHub Pages. It serves as the home for a single-page or small-footprint civic guide focused on Marblehead's 2026 Annual Town Meeting.

The repository is meant to support a workflow that is:

- Fast to publish
- Easy to revise
- Simple to host
- Durable enough to archive
- Clear enough for public readers, not just newsroom staff

Because the site is static, it can be deployed quickly and maintained without a heavy build process. That makes it a strong fit for deadline-driven local reporting and late-stage editorial changes.

## Project Goal

The goal of this guide is to help Marblehead voters and Town Meeting members make sense of the major questions before them.

Town Meeting can be hard to follow even for engaged residents. Warrant articles are often technical. Budget language can be dense. Override questions, zoning proposals and structural changes to town government can be difficult to compare without strong context. This site exists to reduce that friction.

The guide aims to answer questions such as:

- What is being voted on?
- Why does it matter?
- What are the financial or policy consequences?
- Which issues are most significant this year?
- What should residents know before Town Meeting begins?
- What happens after Town Meeting action?

## Editorial Purpose

This repository supports a piece of public-facing local journalism produced by **The Marblehead Independent**. The purpose is not just to post information, but to organize that information in a way that is more useful to the public.

Rather than mirroring town documents, the guide is intended to:

- Summarize the biggest issues
- Add reporting context around major articles
- Explain budget and override questions in plain language
- Help readers understand timelines, procedures and stakes
- Link out to source material and related reporting where useful

The site is meant to preserve the reporting voice and civic focus of The Marblehead Independent while using a format that works well online and is easy to share.

## What The Guide May Cover

Depending on final editorial scope and reporting updates, the guide may include:

- A top-level summary of the 2026 Town Meeting landscape
- Context on the operating budget and revenue picture
- Coverage of override or debt-exclusion questions
- Explanations of major warrant articles
- Zoning and planning proposals
- Committee recommendations and disagreements
- Key dates, deadlines and vote logistics
- Links to original documents, municipal materials and related coverage
- Charts, graphics or visual explainers to make numbers easier to understand

The broader goal is to give residents a single clear guide they can use before attending Town Meeting, discussing warrant articles or voting in follow-up elections.

## Why GitHub Pages

GitHub Pages is a strong fit for this project because it keeps the publishing workflow simple.

Benefits include:

- Free hosting for a static site
- Straightforward version control
- Easy updates when copy changes
- A durable public URL
- Clean handoff between editing and publishing
- A usable archive for future election and Town Meeting cycles

For a newsroom project tied to evolving public debate, those advantages matter. Articles may shift, financial figures may need updates, dates can move and language may be refined right up to publication. GitHub provides a clean way to manage those changes.
